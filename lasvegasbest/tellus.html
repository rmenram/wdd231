Strip

The Las Vegas Strip, a roughly 4.2-mile stretch of South Las Vegas Boulevard, is a world-renowned hub of entertainment, gaming, luxury hotels, and themed resorts. While often associated with the city of Las Vegas, the majority of the Strip actually lies outside the city limits, within the unincorporated towns of Paradise and Winchester in Clark County, Nevada.

Downtown

Downtown Las Vegas, often referred to as DTLV, is the city's historic and original gambling and entertainment district, predating the more famous Strip. It offers a distinctly different vibe from the glamorous mega-resorts of the Strip, being described as grittier, louder, and more focused on the classic Vegas experience.

Outdoors

Beyond the dazzling lights and vibrant nightlife, Las Vegas is surrounded by stunning desert landscapes offering a surprising array of outdoor activities.
